AI in my process

I use AI at every stage, just not the same way twice. Figma Make gets me the first rough shapes, fast and disposable, enough to tell if an idea has legs before I put real time into it. Claude does the research underneath it. Reading support threads, framing what the actual problem is, drafting copy before I have earned the right to write it myself. Then Claude again once a direction is real enough to build on. Back into Figma to polish, using Claude to get the pixels right, spacing, states, the edge cases a first pass always misses.

I've always been a little obsessed with the moment right before things break, and how good design quietly steps in so they don't.

01 / How it started
I started out drawing. Doodles in the margins that turned into film posters for friends' short films in high school. Somewhere in there I realised that making something out of nothing was pretty much the only thing I wanted to do.
02 / The detour that wasn't
College was a happy mess of UX, filmmaking, and photography all at once. Each one kept teaching me the same lesson from a different angle: creativity only counts if it actually works for someone.
03 / What I believe
I'm a hopeless cinephile. World cinema taught me that the best stories pull you in without ever showing the strings, and that's exactly what I want my work to do. When storytelling and usability stop being two separate things, that's when software starts to feel human.
